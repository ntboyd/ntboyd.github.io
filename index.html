About Me.

I study infrastructure decision making and the long-term effects of those decisions with a focus on water-resource systems. I seek to understand the complexity inherent to these systems and to find any general principles that could translate into other areas of study. In short, my research mission is to find extraordinary insights from the ordinary, yet vital infrastructure embedded in our world.

Research Interests.

Infrastructure

Water Resources

Infrastructure systems form an important interface between human activities and the broader natural world. This is especially true for water-resource systems because all life on earth depends on water for survival. Ironically, infrastructure such as water lines, sewers, and dams are easy to take for granted because of this ubiquity. However, practical knowledge of these systems can be translated into extra-ordinary insights related to technological evolution, sustainability, and network science when viewed through the right lens.
