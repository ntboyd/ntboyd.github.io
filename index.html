About Me.

I study infrastructure decision making and the long-term effects of those decisions with a focus on water-resource systems. I seek to understand the complexity inherent to these systems and to find any general principles that could translate into other areas of study. In short, my research mission is to find extraordinary insights from the ordinary, yet vital infrastructure embedded in our world.

Research Interests

Infrastructure

Water Resources

Infrastructure such as water lines, sewers, and dams form an important interface between human activities and the broader natural world. Practical knowledge of these systems can be instructive to broader principles of technological evolution, sustainability, and network science.

Operations Research

Optimization and Game Theory

Operations research studies how organizations make decisions using mathematical models. It has been successfully used to coordinate supply and demand over space and time (e.g., water allocation and distribution to farms and cities).

Sustainability Transitions

Past, Present, and Future

The evolution of technology, such as advanced water treatment, introduces significant uncertainty into how to best adapt the infrastructure inhereted from the past to meet our sustainability goals. Sustainability transitions uses historical case studies and conceptual models to address this challenge.

Complexity

General Systems Principles

Complexity science is an inter-disciplinary field that seeks general principles common to more than one system in nature. For example, sustainable development might share common principles with biological development. Finding these principles could elevate the intellectual merit of work in both fields.
